Short bio

My name is Constantinos Menelaou and I am currently a PhD student in School of Mathematics and Statistics of UCD. Moreover, I am part of the Wave Group in UCD. My research interests include fluid mechanics, numerical simulations and supersonic aerodynamics. Currently, I am studying boulders impacted by breaking waves on coastal areas. I am using Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), in particular the Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) method to simulate coastal boulders. Moreover, I am a programming enthusiast. I am most familiar with C++ and Python which I have been using during my PhD. I also enjoy Rust which I have used for personal projects of mine. Lastly, I enjoy good music, the gym and Formula 1.
